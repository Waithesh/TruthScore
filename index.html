    • Multiple similar channels promoting the same product or course

    Types of YouTube Scams We Detect

    1. Passive Income Scams

    Videos promising "easy money" with "no work required" or "fully automated systems." These typically sell expensive courses ($500-$2,000) or aggressively push affiliate products that don't deliver results.

    2. Dropshipping Course Scams

    Outdated or completely ineffective dropshipping strategies wrapped in professional-looking courses. The market is often oversaturated by the time students implement the "secret" tactics.

    3. Cryptocurrency and Forex Trading Scams

    Get-rich-quick schemes involving trading signals, automated bots, pump-and-dump schemes, or "guaranteed" strategies. The vast majority of followers lose money instead of making it.

    4. Affiliate Marketing Funnels

    Videos designed solely to drive traffic to expensive courses, tools, or coaching programs via undisclosed affiliate links. The "free" advice is worthless without purchasing their promoted products.

    5. Fake Guru Programs

    Self-proclaimed experts selling access to their "exclusive" community, mentorship, or mastermind groups. These programs often provide little actual value beyond recycled generic advice.
