    How to Check if a YouTube Video is a Scam

    The "make money online" industry on YouTube is worth billions, but it is plagued by fake gurus selling dream lifestyles that don't exist. In 2021, YouTube removed the public dislike count, making it harder than ever to spot these scams.

    The Danger of Hidden Metrics

    Scammers rely on social proof. They buy fake views, fake likes, and delete negative comments. Without the dislike button, a video can look completely legitimate even if 50% of viewers hated it. TruthScore restores this transparency by using the ReturnYouTubeDislike API to show you the hidden dislike ratio.

    Common Red Flags We Detect

    Our algorithm scans for specific patterns found in high-risk videos:

    • Unrealistic Promises: Claims of making thousands of dollars in 24 hours with "no work."
    • Urgency Tactics: "Only 5 spots left" or "This video will be deleted soon."
    • Bot Comments: Generic praise like "Great video!" repeated hundreds of times to bury real warnings.
